01 — Identity
Logo System

The DemCon wordmark uses Bebas Neue in two tones: DEM anchors in solid Navy or White, while CON fires in Revolutionary Red — signaling the tension between establishment democracy and radical transformation. A mono-spaced sub-label provides event context.

Logo Rules

Never stretch, rotate, or recolor the wordmark. Minimum size: 120px wide on digital, 25mm on print. Always maintain clear space of 1× the letter height on all sides. The gradient bar (Red → Gold → Sky) may accompany the logo as a decorative element but must not be applied to the letterforms themselves.

06 — Motion & Animation
Motion Principles

Motion references the energy of protest graphics — quick, purposeful, never decorative for its own sake. All transitions feel like turning a page or unfurling a banner.

Entrance
Content slides up and fades in on scroll. Staggered by 80ms between sibling elements.
translateY(20px) → 0 / opacity 0 → 1
Duration
Short: 150ms (micro). Medium: 300ms (page element). Long: 600ms (page transitions).
Short / Medium / Long
Easing
Use ease-out for entrances (like a fist landing). Ease-in-out for transforms. Linear for progress bars.
cubic-bezier(0.0, 0.0, 0.2, 1)
Hero Sequence
On load: wordmark slides up → tagline fades → CTA buttons stagger in → gradient bar draws across bottom.
Total duration: ~1.2s
Hover States
Cards lift 2px. Buttons darken. Track cards scale 1.02. All 200ms ease-out. Never flash or jank.
transform / background / color
Accessibility
All animations respect prefers-reduced-motion. On reduced motion, use instant opacity transitions only.
@media (prefers-reduced-motion)
07 — Iconography
Icon Set

Icons use a consistent 24px grid, 1.5px stroke weight, rounded line caps. The visual language references 20th century movement symbology — bold, legible at small sizes, never ornamental.

09 — Design Philosophy
Principles
Poster Aesthetics
Inspired by 20th century internationalist poster design: bold primaries, flat color, heavy type. Every page should feel like a broadsheet that also converts.
Radical Accessibility
WCAG AA minimum throughout. All color combinations tested for contrast. Full keyboard navigation. Translation-ready structure (RTL layouts supported).
Track Identity
Each of the 8 verticals has its own color signature, icon, and editorial voice — while sharing the common DemCon system for instant coherence.
Protest Energy
The design should feel urgent without feeling aggressive. Every interaction communicates forward movement, solidarity, and possibility.
